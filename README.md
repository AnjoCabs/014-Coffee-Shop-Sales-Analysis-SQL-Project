# 014-Coffee-Shop-Sales-Analysis-SQL-Project

☕ Coffee Shop Sales Analysis Using SQL

📌 Project Overview

This project demonstrates SQL-based exploratory data analysis (EDA) on a coffee shop sales dataset. The objective is to analyze sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, product performance, store profitability, and sales trends using real-world business questions.

🎯 Project Objectives
Analyze overall sales performance.
Evaluate store-level revenue and profitability.
Identify best-selling and low-performing products.
Analyze customer purchasing patterns.
Monitor monthly and yearly sales trends.
Compare weekday and weekend sales.
Rank stores based on revenue.
Demonstrate advanced SQL techniques for retail analytics.

📊 Business Questions Solved
💰 Sales Performance Analysis

1. Sales transactions by year
Filters transactions occurring within a specified year.

2. Total sales revenue
Calculates total revenue using quantity × unit price.

3. Top 10 most expensive products
Identifies products with the highest selling prices.

4. Transactions by store
Retrieves all transactions for a selected store.

5. Number of transactions per store
Measures transaction volume across store locations.

6. Total revenue by store
Ranks stores based on total sales revenue.

🛍️ Product Performance Analysis

7. Average quantity sold by product category
Measures average purchase quantity for each category.

8. Monthly sales trends
Tracks monthly revenue performance over time.

9. Highest revenue-generating product category
Identifies the most profitable product category.

10. Top-selling product categories by sales volume
Ranks categories based on total units sold.

11. Best-selling product type for each store
Determines the most popular product type in every location.

15. Product with the highest revenue contribution
Finds the individual product generating the greatest revenue.

18. Low-performing products
Uses CTEs to identify products with below-average sales volume and revenue.

📅 Time-Based Analysis

12. Weekend vs. weekday sales comparison
Compares sales performance between weekdays and weekends.

13. Peak sales hour
Identifies the busiest hour of the day based on sales.

14. Year-over-year sales growth
Uses window functions to calculate annual sales growth percentages.

19. Holiday season (December) revenue by store
Measures store performance during the holiday shopping season.

🏪 Store Performance Analysis

16. Store with the highest average transaction value
Calculates the average value of customer transactions by store.

17. Revenue contribution by product category
Determines what percentage of total revenue each category contributes.

⭐ Key Takeaways

This project demonstrates how SQL can transform raw transactional data into actionable business insights for the retail and food service industry. By applying advanced SQL concepts such as CTEs, window functions, date functions, and business KPI calculations, it showcases practical skills in sales analytics, revenue reporting, and performance evaluation. The project is ideal for demonstrating proficiency in SQL for Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst, Retail Analyst, and SQL Developer roles.
